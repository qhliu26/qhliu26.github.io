About Me

I'm a third-year PhD student advised by Prof. John Paparrizos at The Ohio State University.
I work on time-series analysis, anomaly detection, and AutoML, with the goal of building intelligent systems that can reliably analyze complex temporal data at scale. My research is organized around three interconnected pillars:
  • Automation: developing end-to-end automated anomaly detection systems that support the full lifecycle of time-series analysis, including model selection, adaptation, and deployment.
  • Generalization: designing time-series foundation models that enable robust few-shot and zero-shot learning, and exploring agentic frameworks that support reasoning and decision-making over temporal data.
  • Reliability: advancing rigorous benchmarking practices and building interpretable anomaly detection methods to ensure trustworthy and transparent time-series analytics.
